Legends

Carlo Ancelotti was noticed by Nils Liedholm in Parma, and he made him the pivot of the Roma side that won a Scudetto and four Italian Cups. He joined AC Milan in 1987. In the year of the first victory of the European Champion Clubs’ Cup, he scored an unforgettable long-range goal against Real Madrid. Ancelotti won two Champions Leagues as a Milan manager. He was a coach at AC Milan for eight years. He retired from playing football at age 33 due to a knee injury.

Twenty-five years in the Milan jersey, 902 official matches played, 26 trophies won In 1997, he inherited the captain’s armband from Baresi and lifted new trophies with Zaccheroni and Ancelotti. The apotheosis came on May 28, 2003, when, after 40 years since Milan’s first European Champion Clubs’ Cup win, he lifted, just like his father Cesare, the Champions League trophy at Old Trafford in Manchester.

Nesta started his career in Lazio and joined Milan in summer 2002. He significantly helped Milan win the most prestigious trophies. Infallible was his penalty kick in the final in Manchester against Juventus, and amazing was his goal to Boca Juniors when Milan became the most successful club in the world

Marco Van Basten joined AC Milan in the summer of 1978. He won Serie A in his debut season with Rossoneri. While in Milan, he won three Ballon d'Or awards and two Serie A top scorer titles. Marco Van Basten bid farewell to football in 1995, at age 33. During his time at AC Milan, he made 201 appearances, scoring 125 goals.

Ruud Gullit joined Ac Milan in the summer of 1978, along with Marco Van Basten. The same year, he won the Ballon d'Or, and while in Milan, he won three league titles: two European Champions Cups, two International Cups, two Ecuadorian Super Cups, and three Italian League Super Cups.

Joining AC Milan in the 1988–89 season, alongside fellow Dutchmen Ruud Gullit and Marco Van Basten, they formed a remarkable trio who carved their names in the club’s history. Frank scored a winning goal against Benfica in the 1990 final and hit a brace in the following Intercontinental Cup against Olimpia Asunción.

Franco Baresi joined AC Milan’s first team during the 1977–78 season. A year later, at the age of eighteen, he won his first league title, putting the star on the jersey for 10 league wins. He finished his football career by counting 719 official matches, 6 league titles, 3 European Champion Clubs' Cups, 2 Intercontinental Cups, 3 European Cup Winners’ Cups, and 4 Italian Supercups. AC Milan retired the number 6 jersey in his honor.

Rui Costa started out in Benfica's youth sector at 9 years of age. A leader of the golden generation that won the Under-20 World Cup on home soil. In 1994, he moved to Fiorentina and played there for 7 seasons. , In 2001, AC Milan signed him. During his three seasons with the Rossoneri, the remarkable number ten won a Scudetto, an Italian Cup, an Italian Supercoppa, a Champions League, and a European Super Cup.

Shevchenko joined Milan in 1999 from Dynamo Kiev, where he won five league titles before moving to Milan. The Ukrainian adjusted to Serie A incredibly quickly, winning the title of league top scorer in his debut season with an impressive 24 goals. Sheva scored the unforgettable winning penalty in the 2003 Champions League final. Only Nordahl managed to score more goals for AC Milan.

Filippo Inzaghi started his football career in the youth sector of Piacenza and played for several clubs, including Leffe, Parma, Atalanta, and Juventus. He arrived in Milan at the peak of his career. "Superpippo" exploded, scoring a plethora of goals, including a decisive brace in the Champions League final revenge in Athens, repeated at the Club World Cup against Boca Juniors. He finished his adventure with Milan as a footballer with 126 goals and 300 official appearances, , along with a long series of trophies.

Born in Suriname, Clarence Seedorf, thanks to his brilliant technique and extraordinary tactical intelligence, is the only player who has won the Champions League with three different clubs. He moved to AC Milan in 2002. In 10 seasons with the Rossoneri, he won numerous trophies in Italy and Europe, two more Champions League titles, two league titles, and a Club World Cup.

Kaka joined AC Milan in 2003 after his amazing success with São Paulo. He immediately helped Milan win their seventeenth league title. The Brazilian's exponential growth culminated in the extraordinary 2006–07 season, when he scored 10 goals and led the team to the Champions League final victory in Athens. Kaka also won the Ballon d'Or and FIFA World Player Awards in 2007, and he also won the European Super Cup and Club World Cup.
