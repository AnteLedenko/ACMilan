San Siro

The stadium was built between August 1925 and September 1926, just 13 months. The first official game was played against Genoa on October 3, 1926.

The stadium is named after the Milanese district in which it is located, San Siro. The stadium is one of the biggest symbols of the city of Milan.

San Siro Stadium has 75.817 seats; it is the biggest stadium in Italy. The structure, featuring high standards of comfort and safety, is classified as UEFA's Category 4.
