AC Milan won their first Champions League against Benfica in the 1962â€“63 season. The final was played at Wembley Stadium. Milan won 2-1, with Jose Altafini scoring two goals in the 58th and 66th minutes of the game.

Season 1968/69: AC Milan won their second Champions League title against Ajax with a 4-1 win, with Angelo Sarmoni scoring in the 66th and Pierino Prati in the 7th, 39th, and 74th minutes. The second title was won at the Santiago Bernabeu Stadium in Madrid.

In the 1988/89 season, they won against Steaua Bucharest 4-0. Ruud Gullit and Marco Van Basten each scored two goals in the 18th, 27th, 39th, and 47th minutes of the game, leading Milan to their third title. The final was played at Barcelona Camp Nou.

Following the 1989/90 season, AC Milan won again, this time against Benfica. The only goal in the game was scored by Frank Rijkaard in the 68th minute of the game. The game was played at the Ernst Happel Stadium in Vienna, where Milan secured their fourth title as champions of Europe.

The 1993/94 Champions League finals were played in Athens at the Spyros Olympic Stadium, where AC Milan convincingly won against Barcelona 4-0. The goal scorers were Daniel Massaro in the 22nd and 45th, Dejan Savicevic in the 47th, and Marcel Desailly in the 58th.

AC Milan won their sixth title against their Italian rival Juventus. The 2002/03 final was played at Old Trafford in Manchester. Milan was celebrating their 6th title with a 3-2 win. The goalscorers from Milan were Sergio, Alessandro Nesta, and Andriy Shevchenko.

In season 2006/07, AC Milan got their revenge for losing the final to Liverpool two years earlier. Milan won 2-1 against Liverpool in Athens at the Spyros Olympic Stadium, and with this win, they lifted the Cup of Champions of Europe for the 7th time. Goals for Milan were scored by Filippo Inzaghi in the 45th and 82nd minutes of the game.
